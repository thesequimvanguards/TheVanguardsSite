About Us

The Vanguards is an acapella group originally founded in the summer of 2025 in Sequim, Washington. What began as a local group of dedicated young vocalists has developed into a distinctive musical identity rooted in classical repertoire. As of Fall 2026, The Vanguards is expanding beyond Sequim with the formation of The Wesleyan Vanguards in Bloomington, Illinois, at Illinois Wesleyan University, with the Collegiate branch founded by Hayden Rayburn. This new chapter extends the ensemble’s mission into a collegiate setting, continuing the group’s commitment to artistic excellence, bold repertoire, and community‑centered music‑making.

During the 2026 Solo and Ensemble Regional Contest, The Sequim Vanguards earned first place under their primary ensemble name, while their secondary group, the Lavish Lavender Lads, secured third place. As regional finalists, The Sequim Vanguards will advance to the State Level, where they will compete against the top vocal ensembles in Washington. This accomplishment highlights the group’s musical excellence, dedication, and growing presence within the state’s choral community.

As the Sequim Vanguards celebrate the accomplishments of our graduating seniors, Hayden Rayburn, Jac Curtin, Ryan Stites, and Max Souza, we also look ahead with confidence and excitement. Their dedication, musicianship, and leadership shaped the ensemble’s identity and set a high standard for future generations. With their graduation, the group enters a new chapter under the emerging leadership of Seth Baros and Boden Cowgill. Their vision, talent, and commitment ensure that the Sequim Vanguards will continue to grow, innovate, and uphold the musical excellence established by the founding members.

Meet the Members

Seth Baros is a freshman at Sequim High School and a Tenor 1 in The Sequim Vanguards. He is interested in singing, playing violin for church, and building 3D ships and other models. He enjoys spending time with my family and looks forward to progressing in his singing and maybe looking into a career with music!

Tenor 1 – Aidan Danielson

Aidan Danielson is a Junior at Sequim High School and Tenor 1 in The Sequim Vanguards. He has been in the Sequim Vocal Ensemble since freshman year and has since then placed 1st three times with Sequim Vocal Ensemble and Regional Solo and Ensemble, placed 1st twice with Sequim Men's Ensemble at Regional Solo and ensemble, placed 3rd with Sequim Men’s Ensemble at State Solo and Ensemble Championship, and placed 3rd at regional Solo and Ensemble as a tenor soloist. Music has been a central part of Aidan's life since 6th grade to present! He is excited to continue with the Sequim Vanguards in the future!

Tenor 2 – Hayden Rayburn

Hayden Rayburn is the founder of The Vanguards and a senior at Sequim High School. He will study Vocal Performance at the Illinois Wesleyan University School of Music after graduation. Hayden is passionate about inspiring others through classical music and hopes to continue his training at a top conservatory for his master’s degree. He is especially drawn to Norwegian and Scandinavian repertoire, taking inspiration from composers like Edvard Grieg and Jean Sibelius as he prepares for a future in international performance.

Tenor 2 – Lincoln Terwilger

I’m Lincoln, a junior at Sequim high school and a Tenor 2 in The Sequim Vanguards, and alongside singing I love to write and research things like astronomy and the ocean, I’m really looking forward to enhancing my vocal abilities with this group and hopefully grace those around us with enjoyable music!

Baritone – Boden Cowgill

Boden Cowgill, a member of the Vanguards, is currently a Junior at Sequim High School. He aims to engage in musical theatre and the arts of entertainment professionally in his future. Being apart of the Vanguards blesses him with wonderful opportunities to learn and achieve new goals musically. He is extremely thrilled to see the group flourish!

Baritone – Jac Curtin

Jac Curtin is a senior at Sequim High School and one of the earliest members of The Vanguards, having joined back when the ensemble operated under a different name. Although he began his musical journey only in his junior year, Jac quickly immersed himself in choir, concerts, and competitions. He performed with the Sequim Men’s Ensemble, which placed 3rd in Washington State at the 2025 Solo & Ensemble Festival, and he is currently a member of Sequim High School’s top choir, Vocal Ensemble, as well as an active participant in the school’s choir officer program. After graduation, Jac plans to pursue trade school with the goal of becoming an electrician, carrying with him the discipline, teamwork, and joy he found through music.

Bass – Ryan Stites

I’m Ryan Stites, a Senior at Sequim High School and have been in the high school’s Vocal ensemble for two years and to our state competition as a bass soloist both of those years. I have loved music from a very young age singing in my church choir, also I am very excited to continue this next year at Concordia St. Paul where I have committed. I am pursuing a bachelor’s in theology and plan to become a pastor where I pray God will utilize my musical gifts to his glory.

Bass – Max Suaza

Max Souza is a senior at Sequim High School and a bass in The Sequim Vanguards. Music has been a central part of his life since middle school, where he first began in band and quickly discovered his passion for performing. In high school, Max advanced into the top jazz band, continuing to grow as both a musician and ensemble member. During his junior year, Max was introduced to the Sequim choir program at a state competition, an experience that inspired him to join choir soon after. Since then, he has performed in concerts, recitals, and solo opportunities, often drawing on his trombone background to support the high school choir. At the 2026 Regional Solo & Ensemble Festival, Max sang with the SHS Men’s Ensemble, earning 1st place. He looks forward to continuing his work with The Vanguards throughout the rest of the year. After graduation, Max plans to pursue music further, either independently or through formal study after taking a gap year.
